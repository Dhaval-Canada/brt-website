Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance traditions in India[citation needed]. It was nurtured in the temples of India since ancient times.
Bharata Natyam comes from the words Bhava(Expression), Raga (Music), Tala(Rhythm) and Natya (Classic Indian Musical Theater).
It is one of eight forms of Indian classical dance forms, (the others being Kathak, Kuchipudi, Odissi, Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, Manipuri and Sattriya) and it expresses Indian religious themes and spiritual ideas, particularly of Shaivism, Vaishnavism and Shaktism.

Description of Bharatanatyam by 2nd century CE is noted in the ancient temple sculptures of 6th to 9th century CE suggest it was a well refined performance art by the mid-1st millennium CE. Bharatanatyam remained exclusive to Hindu temples through the 19th century.It was banned by the colonial British government in 1910,but the Indian community protested against the ban and expanded it outside the temples in the 20th century.Today,it is one of the most popular and widely performed dance styles and is practiced by many dancers all over the world.


4 Major Styles Of Bharatnatyam are Melattur Style, Pandanallur Style, Vazhuvoor Style & Kalakshetra Style.
